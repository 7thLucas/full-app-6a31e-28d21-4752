# Product Overview — نصوص (Nusus)

## Product Name
**نصوص** (Nusus) — Arabic Reading Text Discovery Platform

## Tagline
Find the right Arabic reading text, for every lesson.

## What It Is
A focused web application that searches the internet for diverse Arabic reading texts, filters them by genre and text type, and calibrates results to the preparatory secondary school level (grades 7–9 / الثانوي الإعدادي). Teachers find lesson-ready reading materials in under 60 seconds; students access enriching, level-appropriate texts on demand.

## The Problem
Arabic language teachers at the preparatory secondary level spend an estimated 2–3 hours per week manually browsing scattered internet sources — educational portals, literary sites, and general web pages — searching for reading texts appropriate in type and level. No dedicated tool exists that aggregates, categorizes, and filters Arabic reading texts by genre and reading level for الثانوي الإعدادي students, forcing educators to sift through irrelevant and off-level results.

## The Solution
A single-purpose text discovery application with smart search by text type, preparatory secondary level calibration, multi-source internet aggregation, inline text preview, personal save and bookmark functionality, and keyword/topic search — so teachers and students find the right text fast.

## Target Users

### Primary — Arabic Language Teachers
Teachers at preparatory secondary schools (grades 7–9, ages 12–15) who need fast access to varied, level-appropriate reading texts for lesson planning, classroom exercises, homework, and reading enrichment.

### Secondary — Preparatory Secondary Students
Students (ages 12–15) seeking independent reading practice across different text genres and types.

### Supporting — Educational Coordinators
Staff who oversee reading curriculum and need to source texts across multiple text types systematically.

## Text Types Covered (5 Categories)
1. **Narrative** (النصوص السردية) — story-based and narrative passages
2. **Descriptive** (النصوص الوصفية) — observational and descriptive writing
3. **Argumentative / Persuasive** (النصوص الحجاجية) — structured argument and persuasion
4. **Expository / Informational** (النصوص المعلوماتية) — explanatory and informational texts
5. **Literary / Poetic** (النصوص الأدبية) — literary excerpts, classical and modern poetry

## Core Features
1. **Text-Type Search** — one-click filtering by any of the 5 text genre categories
2. **Level Calibration** — all results filtered and scored for preparatory secondary reading standard (grades 7–9)
3. **Internet-Wide Aggregation** — searches and ranks results across multiple educational, literary, and cultural Arabic-language websites
4. **Inline Text Preview** — read the complete text within the app before saving or sharing
5. **Save & Organize** — personal library for teachers to bookmark and collect texts by lesson, unit, or topic
6. **Keyword & Topic Search** — find texts by theme or subject keyword (e.g. البيئة, الوطن, العلم)

## Volume Assumptions (for projection purposes)
- Estimated teacher time spent searching for reading texts: **2 hours per week** per teacher (assumption — adjust to reality)
- School year length: **40 instructional weeks**
- Typical deployment: **5 Arabic language teachers** per school (assumption — adjust to school size)
- Estimated search frequency: **4 searches per teacher per week** (assumption)
- Time saved per successful search: **30 minutes** (derived: 2 hrs/week ÷ 4 searches/week)

## Positioning
A curriculum-aligned discovery tool — not a general reading platform or content publisher. The app surfaces what already exists on the internet and makes it instantly findable by type and reading level, filling a gap no existing search engine or educational portal addresses specifically for الثانوي الإعدادي.

## Brand Tone
Clear, professional, educational. Warm and efficient — built for busy teachers who value their time, and for students who benefit from exposure to varied, quality Arabic reading.

## Strategic Principles
1. **Level before volume** — results must fit the preparatory secondary reading standard; fewer quality matches beat more irrelevant ones.
2. **Type diversity** — every session should surface multiple text types; variety is a core promise.
3. **Speed to value** — a teacher should find a usable, level-appropriate text within 60 seconds of opening the app.
